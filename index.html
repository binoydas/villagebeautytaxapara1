Introduction

Welcome to Taxapara, a hidden gem nestled in the state of Tripura, India. With a geographical area of 10,491 km², this predominantly hilly region (60%) is surrounded on three sides by the deltaic basin of Bangladesh.

Taxapara is situated between 22°57’ and 24°32’N latitude, and 91°10’ and 92°20’E longitude, with the Tropic of Cancer passing through it. This village holds a special place in the south-western extremity of the North-East region of the country.

Sharing its borders with Bangladesh, Assam, and Mizoram, Taxapara's international border with Bangladesh spans 856 km, characterized by an open and porous terrain. The lush forests in the state primarily comprise tropical evergreen, semi-evergreen, and moist deciduous varieties.

A significant portion is adorned with bamboo brakes, forming a unique "Sub-climax" ecosystem resulting from age-old shifting cultivation practices. Here are some key statistics about the forest resources:

  • (a) Forest Area: 6294.287 km²
  • (b) Percentage (%) of forest area to geographical area (10,491 km²): 59.99%

Landscape of Taxapara

Enveloped by the natural splendor of Tripura, Taxapara is a picturesque village that boasts a diverse and captivating landscape. Spread over an area of 10,491 square kilometers, Taxapara is predominantly hilly, with approximately 60% of its terrain characterized by undulating slopes and verdant peaks.

Located between 22°57’ and 24°32’N latitude and 91°10’ and 92°20’E longitude, Taxapara is intersected by the Tropic of Cancer, lending it a unique geographical significance. The village is cocooned by a deltaic basin on three sides, an area teeming with lush greenery and fertile plains that extend into the heart of Bangladesh.

The forests of Taxapara are its crown jewel, comprising mainly of tropical evergreen, semi-evergreen, and moist deciduous varieties. Bamboo brakes, a result of centuries-old shifting cultivation practices, virtually form a "Sub-climax" ecosystem, adding to the village's rich biodiversity.

The surrounding areas offer breathtaking vistas of majestic mountains, winding rivers, and sprawling forests, inviting both adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts to explore the hidden treasures that Taxapara has to offer.

Wildlife in Taxapara

Taxapara is a haven for biodiversity enthusiasts. Nestled in the heart of Tripura, this village boasts a rich tapestry of wildlife, thriving in its diverse ecosystem. Here are some of the fascinating species that call Taxapara home:

Bengal Tigers

These majestic creatures, apex predators of the region, roam the dense forests of Taxapara. Their presence underscores the health and vitality of the local ecosystem.

Hoolock Gibbons

With their distinctive calls echoing through the treetops, Hoolock gibbons are an iconic sight in Taxapara. These agile primates swing gracefully among the branches, adding a touch of enchantment to the village.

Indian Elephants

The gentle giants of Taxapara, Indian elephants, are often spotted in the quieter, forested areas. Their presence is a testament to the preservation of their natural habitat.

Rare Bird Species

Taxapara's skies are adorned with a vibrant array of avian wonders. From the resplendent plumage of tropical birds to the melodious calls of migratory species, birdwatchers will find their paradise here.

Diverse Reptiles

The forest floor and streams of Taxapara are inhabited by a variety of reptiles, including snakes, lizards, and terrapins. These often elusive creatures play crucial roles in the local ecosystem.

Exploring Taxapara offers a unique opportunity to witness these creatures in their natural habitat. Conservation efforts and sustainable practices ensure that this vibrant wildlife continues to thrive for generations to come.
